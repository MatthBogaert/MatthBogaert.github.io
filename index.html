About Me

I am currently working as an Associate Professor of Data Analytics at the Marketing, Innovation, and Organization Department (Research Group Data Analytics) at Ghent University. Besides that, I am also Visiting Professor at the University of Namur and a member of the CVAMO research group at Flanders Make.

Prior to joining Ghent University, I worked as an Assistant Professor at the University of Edinburgh Business School and as a Postdoctoral Researcher at KU Leuven. I studied Business Engineering and received a PhD in Business Economics at Ghent University in 2018.

I have taught a wide range of data analytics courses ranging from basic statistics and database management to advanced predictive analytics and social media & web analytics. My research focuses on applications of descriptive, predictive and prescriptive analytics in social media, customer relationship management, hospitality, sports, and production & manufacturing. My research has been published in several well-known international journals such as the European Journal of Operational Research, Machine Learning, Omega, Decision Sciences, among others.

Research

I investigate data-driven decision support systems — end-to-end analytical pipelines that turn raw, heterogeneous data into operational business decisions. Central methodological themes across my work are cost-sensitive learning, explainable AI, ensemble methods, and NLP, applied across a broad range of domains.

Application areas with a strong publication track include customer relationship management (churn prediction, customer acquisition, cross-selling), sports analytics (professional cycling performance and talent identification), social media analytics (rumor detection, crisis communication), and credit scoring. More recent work extends into production and manufacturing environments, with a focus on predictive maintenance, fault detection, and supply chain decision-making.

I am a member of CVAMO at Flanders Make, the strategic research centre for the manufacturing industry in Flanders. For a full overview of projects, see my UGent project page.

Projects

REMASC

Predictive and prescriptive models for managing the reverse remanufacturing value chain, enabling smarter decisions on returns, repair, and resource allocation in industrial settings.

COSTLEAP

Cost-driven machine learning for production environments, building models that optimise maintenance strategies by directly accounting for the economic consequences of decisions.

AGEAS

Automated generation and lifecycle cost analysis of production system designs under uncertain multi-product demand, combining simulation and data-driven forecasting to support system-level planning decisions.

Research Group

Data Analytics – Ghent University

I work at the Research Group Data Analytics at the Faculty of Economics and Business Administration of Ghent University. The group engages in teaching and research on the use of data to improve and optimize business processes, based on techniques such as statistics, data mining, and machine learning (e.g., deep learning and reinforcement learning) and optimization.

Research Portal

CVAMO – Flanders Make

Cost and Value Analytics, Models & Optimization (CVAMO) groups 10 professors and about 28 pre- or postdoctoral researchers from different departments at the Faculty of Economics and Business Administration (Ghent University). CVAMO is a core lab in the Strategic Research Center Flanders Make, that groups research labs from all Flemish universities to support companies in the manufacturing industry in developing and optimizing products and production processes.
